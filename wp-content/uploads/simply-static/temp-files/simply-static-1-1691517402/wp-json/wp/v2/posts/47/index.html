Virat Kohli<\/strong>\u00a0(Hindi pronunciation:\u00a0<\/small>[\u028b\u026a\u02c8\u027e\u0251\u02d0\u0288 \u02c8ko\u02d0\u0266li]<\/a>\u00a0(<\/a>listen<\/a>); born 5 November 1988) is an Indian\u00a0international cricketer<\/a>\u00a0and the former captain of the\u00a0Indian national cricket team<\/a>. Widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport,[4]<\/a><\/sup>\u00a0he plays for\u00a0Royal Challengers Bangalore<\/a>\u00a0in the\u00a0IPL<\/a>\u00a0and\u00a0Delhi<\/a>\u00a0in domestic cricket. Kohli holds numerous records in his career which includes scoring most runs in\u00a0T20 internationals<\/a>\u00a0and IPL, fastest to reach 10,000\u00a0ODI<\/a>\u00a0runs. In 2020, the\u00a0International Cricket Council\u00a0named him the\u00a0male cricketer of the decade<\/a>. Kohli has also contributed to India’s successes, including winning the\u00a02011 World Cup<\/a>\u00a0and the\u00a02013 Champions trophy<\/a>. He is among the only four Indian cricketers who has played 500 matches for India.[5]<\/a><\/sup><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Born and raised in New Delhi, Kohli trained at the West Delhi Cricket Academy<\/a> and started his youth career with the Delhi Under-15 team. He made his international debut in 2008 and quickly became a key player in the ODI team and later made his Test debut in 2011. In 2013, Kohli reached the number one spot in the ICC rankings<\/a> for ODI batsmen for the first time. During 2014 T20 World Cup<\/a>, he set a record for the most runs scored in the tournament. In 2018, he achieved yet another milestone, becoming the world’s top-ranked Test batsman, making him the only Indian cricketer to hold the number one spot in all three formats of the game. His form continued in 2019, when he became the first player to score 20,000 international runs in a single decade. In 2021, Kohli made the decision to step down as the captain of the Indian national team for T20Is, following the T20 World Cup and in early 2022 he stepped down as the captain of the Test team as well.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
